What is a Lararium?

A lararium was the spiritual center of a Roman home - a shrine where families honored protective spirits and household gods through daily rituals and offerings.

Household Gods & Spirits

The foundation of Roman domestic religion centered on spirits that protected the home and family:

  • Lares: Guardian spirits of the household and family
  • Penates: Gods of the pantry who guarded the family's food stores
  • Vesta: Goddess of the hearth and home
  • Genius: The personal guardian spirit of the paterfamilias

These household deities formed the core of daily Roman worship, with families making regular offerings at their lararium.

×

The Major Roman Deities

Beyond household spirits, Romans honored a pantheon of major gods who governed all aspects of life:

  • Jupiter: King of the gods, god of sky and thunder
  • Juno: Queen of the gods, protector of women and marriage
  • Minerva: Goddess of wisdom, strategy, and crafts
  • Mars: God of war, agriculture, and protector of Rome
  • Venus: Goddess of love, beauty, and fertility
  • Mercury: Messenger god, commerce, and communication

Romans honored these gods both in public ceremonies and through their household lararia.

Roman Worship Rituals

Roman religious practice combined daily household rituals with periodic public ceremonies:

  • Morning Offerings: Daily offerings to household gods at the lararium
  • Libations: Pouring wine, oil, or other liquids as offerings
  • Prayers: Formal invocations following specific patterns
  • Festival Observances: Participating in public religious festivals
  • Votive Offerings: Gifts promised to gods in exchange for favors

Lararium Meum guides you through authentic Roman rituals for both household and major deities.

Roman Religious Calendar

The Roman year was structured around a rich calendar of religious observances:

  • Kalends: The first day of each month, dedicated to Juno
  • Nones: The 5th or 7th day of the month
  • Ides: The 13th or 15th day, dedicated to Jupiter
  • Major Festivals: Dozens of religious festivals throughout the year

Lararium Meum includes the complete Roman religious calendar with observances for both household and state religion.

From Home to State: The Complete Roman Experience

Roman religion wasn't divided between household and state worship - they were interconnected parts of a whole. The paterfamilias (head of household) served as priest for his family, while also participating in public religious life.
